## A. Strongest Positioning Options

Below are 5 distinct positioning angles, each grounded in her real background. The goal is not to pick all of them, but to identify the 1-2 that feel most authentic and marketable.

### Option 1: Clinical-to-Product Bridge
**Label:** "Physician → Product Thinking for Healthcare"

**One-liner:** Clinician with deep medical knowledge who understands product development, operations, and health communication—built to translate clinical needs into scalable health solutions.

**Who it appeals to:** Early-stage healthtech founders, Series A–B companies building clinical-facing products, care coordination startups, patient engagement platforms.

**Why it's credible:**
- Genuine medical training (residency, clinical work) + proven ability to run a business (6+ years managing retail operations: hiring, pricing, payroll, negotiations)
- R&D experience (thermoplastic materials project: literature review, sourcing, prototyping) shows product iteration thinking
- Not a "clinical consultant who codes on weekends"—she has real operational and innovation experience

**Strongest angle:** She bridges the gap between clinical rigor and startup execution.

### Recommendation: Strongest 1–2 Positioning

**Primary:** **Option 1 (Clinical-to-Product Bridge)** is her strongest and most differentiated positioning. It's broad enough to apply to most health startups, but specific enough to be credible. It plays to her unusual combination of clinical training + business operations + R&D. This should be her default.

**Secondary:** **Option 2 (Medical Content & Health Communication)** is her best-differentiated positioning if she wants a more specific lane. The 2,200+ followers is *not* a vanity metric—it's proof of concept. It appeals to a narrower but concrete set of companies.

**Tertiary:** **Option 4 (Health Operations)** is valuable if she's interested in care delivery, telemedicine scaling, or operations-focused roles. Less glamorous than "product," but solid and needed.
