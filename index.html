About

Everything else grew from there.

I graduated from First Pavlov State Medical University in Saint Petersburg, did my residency in internal medicine and gastroenterology, and worked in both emergency admissions and private practice. My degree is homologated in Spain, so I can practice across the EU.

I speak three languages (Russian, Spanish, English), have lived in 6+ countries across 4 continents, and I’m more comfortable explaining how your gut works than writing an “about me” section.

Philosophy

What I believe about medicine and communication

Evidence over authority

A claim doesn’t become true because a doctor said it. It becomes true when the data supports it. I cite sources, flag study limitations, and say “we don’t know yet” when that’s the honest answer.

Clarity is kindness

If a patient leaves my office confused, that’s on me, not them. The same goes for writing. I spend 10 minutes explaining that tomatoes and bell peppers can be eaten together if that’s what it takes to undo a myth.

Doctors are people too

I’ve been on antidepressants, I’ve been scared before surgery, and I once skipped an entire ophthalmology module because cataract surgery footage was too much for me. Being open about this makes me a better doctor, not a worse one.
