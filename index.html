Philosophy

What I believe about medicine and communication

Evidence over authority

A claim doesn’t become true because a doctor said it. It becomes true when the data supports it. I cite sources, flag study limitations, and say “we don’t know yet” when that’s the honest answer.

Clarity is kindness

If a patient leaves my office confused, that’s on me, not them. The same goes for writing. I’ll spend 10 minutes explaining that eating after 6 pm won’t make you gain weight — or untangling things the internet told you about food — if that’s what it takes to undo a myth.

Doctors are people too

I’ve been on antidepressants. I’ve been scared before surgery. Being open about this makes me a better doctor, not a worse one. And it makes the information I share more honest.
